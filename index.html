About

Kara Rudolph is an Assistant Professor in Mailman School of Public Health at Columbia University in New York.

Her current work, funded by a NIDA K99/R00, is developing methods to transport mediation effects from one population to another and apply them to understand how school characteristics mediate relationships between neighborhood factors and adolescent drug use.

She graduated with a PhD (Epidemiology) and MHS (Biostatistics) from the Johns Hopkins Bloomberg School of Public Health in 2014.

Interests

  • Causal inference
  • Transportation of mediation effects
